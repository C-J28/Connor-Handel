About Me

I am a Computer & Technology Security student at Drexel University with a passion for hands-on engineering and problem solving. From an early age, I was drawn to understanding how things work — a curiosity that led me from building forts in the woods to building circuits at my workbench.

My journey into computing started with a Raspberry Pi Model A kit, and that spark has grown into deep experience across embedded platforms including Arduino, ESP32/8266, and multiple generations of Raspberry Pi. I enjoy working at the intersection of hardware and software, where creative solutions meet real-world constraints.

Beyond embedded systems, I have developed skills in 3D modeling and printing, CNC machining, welding, and electronics prototyping. I run a Proxmox-based home lab for networking experiments and maintain several personal projects on GitHub.
